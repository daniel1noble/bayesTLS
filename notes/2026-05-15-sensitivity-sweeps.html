Sensitivity sweeps comparing the joint 4PL and the classical two-stage TDT estimator

Author

Daniel Noble (with Claude assistance)

Published

May 15, 2026

1 Abstract

We compare the joint Bayesian 4-parameter logistic (4PL) estimator developed in this paper against the classical two-stage thermal-death-time (TDT) pipeline. We hold the data-generating process fixed at a beta-binomial 4PL surface with temperature acting only on the midpoint, and sweep three factors one at a time: (A) the temperature slope on the upper asymptote u; (B) the value of u at the centring temperature T̄; (C) the (temperatures × durations × replicates per cell) design grid. Each sweep cell uses 1000 simulated experiments with N=500 replicates of the inferential workflow. We report bias, RMSE, 95% interval coverage, and the full per-simulation distribution of point estimates for \(z\) and \(\mathrm{CTmax_{1hr}}\), each with Monte Carlo standard errors.

2 Background

Two estimators are in common use in the TDT literature for extracting \(z\) (the temperature increment for a ten-fold change in \(\text{LT}_{50}\)) and \(\text{CT}_{\max,1\text{h}}\) from a temperature × duration × survival assay:

  • Joint Bayesian 4PL — a single non-linear model fitted to all the data simultaneously, allowing all four 4PL sub-parameters (lower asymptote \(\ell\), upper asymptote \(u\), slope \(k\), midpoint \(m\)) to vary with temperature. This is the estimator developed here, implemented in the bayesTLS R package via brms with a beta-binomial(identity) likelihood.
  • Classical two-stage — Stage 1 fits a per-temperature binomial GLM with logit link on \(\log_{10}\) duration; Stage 2 fits an unweighted OLS regression of the Stage-1 \(\log_{10}\mathrm{LT_{50}}\) on temperature, with 95% Wald CIs from the delta method.

A previous note (baseline + 3 categorical DGPs) found that two-stage carries a bias of roughly 0.5 °C on \(z\) and \(\mathrm{CTmax_{1hr}}\) at the dose-response parameters used there, but that the cross-DGP differences were too small to actually shift either method’s point estimates. The present run sweeps each factor across a wider, continuous range to characterise when the methods diverge.

3 Methods

3.1 Estimators

Both estimators take the same input — a long-format table with columns (temperature, duration, n_trials, n_survivors) — and return a point estimate and a 95% interval for \(z\) and \(\mathrm{CTmax_{1hr}}\).

Joint Bayesian 4PL. bayesTLS::fit_4pl() builds the non-linear brms formula

\[ p(T, t) \;=\; \ell^*(T) + \bigl[u^*(T) - \ell^*(T)\bigr] \big/ \bigl[1 + \exp\!\bigl(k(T)(\log_{10} t - m(T))\bigr)\bigr], \]

where the asymptotes \(\ell^*, u^*\) are mapped from unconstrained parameters via a disjoint-bounds reparameterisation that keeps the 4PL output inside \((\ell_\text{min}, u_\text{max})\) so the beta_binomial(link="identity") family is safe. Every sub-parameter (\(\ell, u, k, m\)) carries a centred-temperature slope. We use the default priors from bayesTLS::make_4pl_priors() (weakly informative, chosen for proportion-scale data in \([0, 1]\)). Sampling: 2 chains × 2,000 iterations (half discarded as warm-up), cmdstanr backend, adapt_delta = 0.9, max_treedepth = 12. We extract \(z\) and \(\mathrm{CTmax_{1hr}}\) from the posterior via extract_tdt() and report the posterior median and 2.5 % / 97.5 % quantiles (1000 posterior draws kept per simulation).

Classical two-stage. fit_two_stage_classical() does the textbook recipe: for each temperature, fit glm(cbind(n_surv, n_dead) ~ log10(t), family = binomial("logit")), read off \(\hat{\log_{10}\mathrm{LT_{50}}} = -\hat\beta_0/\hat\beta_1\) with delta-method SE; then lm(log10_LT50 ~ T) and read off \(z = -1/\hat\beta_1^{(2)}\) and \(\mathrm{CTmax_{1hr}} = (\log_{10}\!60 - \hat\beta_0^{(2)})/\hat\beta_1^{(2)}\), with 95 % Wald CIs from the delta method on the Stage-2 covariance. A simulated dataset is two-stage success only when all Stage-1 fits returned a finite negative slope and the Stage-2 slope was finite and negative.

3.2 Data-generating process

Simulated datasets come from a beta-binomial 4PL with the same disjoint-bounds reparameterisation as the joint fit. For each cell \((T_i, t_j)\) in the design grid (see Designs) and each replicate cup, the per-cup sample size \(N\) is drawn from a discrete uniform on \(\{10, 11, \dots, 20\}\) and the observed survivor count from a beta-binomial with mean \(p(T_i, t_j)\) and dispersion \(\phi = 5\). The full DGP parameter set is

symbol default notes
\(\ell\) 0.05 lower asymptote at \(T = \bar T\)
\(u\) 0.92 upper asymptote at \(T = \bar T\) — swept in B
\(k\) 8 slope
\(\beta_0\) 1.5 \(m(\bar T) = \log_{10}\) LT₅₀ at \(\bar T\)
\(\beta_1\) −0.15 \(\partial m / \partial T\)
\(\bar T\) 34 °C centring temperature
\(\phi\) 5 beta-binomial dispersion
\(u_{\beta_1}\) 0 \(\partial u / \partial T\) — swept in A
\(\ell_{\beta_1}\) 0 \(\partial \ell / \partial T\)
\(k_{\beta_1}\) 0 \(\partial k / \partial T\)

The truth values of \(z\) and \(\mathrm{CTmax_{1hr}}\) used to score bias are the OLS targets — the slope and (60-min) intercept of \(\log_{10}\!\mathrm{LT_{50}}\) as a function of \(T\) at the design temperatures, computed analytically from the DGP. This is the quantity both estimators are trying to estimate.

3.3 Sweeps

Three sweeps, each varying one factor while holding the other two at their defaults.

knitr::kable(sweep_table)

The full design is 5 temperatures × 6 durations = 30 cells per dataset (temperatures 30, 32, 34, 36, 38 °C; durations 1, 5, 15, 45, 135, 405 min). The sparse design drops to 3 temperatures × 4 durations = 12 cells (30, 34, 38 °C; 1, 15, 135, 405 min). Replication multiplies the cell count by n_reps.

3.4 Sample sizes

Each sweep cell is N = 500 simulated experiments. We chose 500 to keep the total queue under a day of compute while keeping Monte Carlo standard errors on the four headline summaries (mean bias, RMSE, coverage, paired difference) inside pre-specified thresholds (bias MCSE < 0.05 °C, coverage MCSE < 0.015). Cells failing either threshold would be re-run with an additional 500 sims appended (resume-safe, per-sim RDS storage).

Total simulated experiments: \(6 \times 500 + 5 \times 500 + 6 \times 500 = 8500\).

3.5 Performance metrics

For each (sweep × cell × method × quantity) we report

  • Mean bias — sample mean of \((\hat{\theta}_i - \theta_\text{OLS})\) across the 500 sims;
  • RMSE — sample root-mean-square of \((\hat{\theta}_i - \theta_\text{OLS})\);
  • 95 % interval coverage — fraction of sims whose 95 % interval contains \(\theta_\text{OLS}\);
  • Median 95 % interval width;

each with its Monte Carlo standard error. We also report the full distribution of the 500 per-simulation point estimates (violin plots, with the OLS truth marked).

Per-simulation raw outputs (point estimate, CI bounds, success flag for each method × quantity; plus 1000 posterior draws per simulation for \(z\), \(\mathrm{CTmax_{1hr}}\), \(T_\text{crit}\) for the joint method) are kept in output/sim_twostage/raw/<label>/sim_NNNNN.rds.

5 MCSE adequacy

parts <- list()
if (nrow(summary_a) > 0)
  parts$A <- summary_a |> mutate(sweep = "A",
                                  level = as.character(u_beta1))
if (nrow(summary_b) > 0)
  parts$B <- summary_b |> mutate(sweep = "B",
                                  level = as.character(u_0))
if (nrow(summary_c) > 0)
  parts$C <- summary_c |> mutate(sweep = "C",
                                  level = as.character(condition))

if (length(parts) == 0L) {
  cat("*No cells loaded yet.*")
} else {
  all_sum <- bind_rows(parts) |>
    mutate(bias_ok = mcse_bias < 0.05,
           cov_ok  = mcse_cov  < 0.015,
           both_ok = bias_ok & cov_ok) |>
    select(sweep, level, method, quantity,
           mcse_bias, bias_ok, mcse_cov, cov_ok, both_ok)
  knitr::kable(all_sum, digits = 4)
}

6 Limitations

  • DGP scope. Sweep A varies only u_beta1; sweeps along ell_beta1 and k_beta1 are deferred. Joint interactions (e.g. simultaneous u_beta1 and k_beta1) untested.
  • Joint-method prior sensitivity. All cells use make_4pl_priors() defaults. The joint estimator’s bias/coverage will change if priors are tightened or loosened.
  • Two-stage implementation. We benchmark the textbook unweighted-OLS, 2-parameter logit-GLM-per-temperature version. Alternative implementations (SE-weighted Stage 2; 4-parameter dose-response per temperature) would give different numbers.
  • Single truth definition. Both methods are scored against the OLS-derived target at the design temperatures. A parametric truth (\(z = -1/\beta_1\)) would give different per-cell bias numbers in Sweep A but the same comparison logic.

7 Data availability

All raw simulation outputs are kept under output/sim_twostage/:

  • raw/<label>/sim_NNNNN.rds — one file per simulation, containing the row summary (point estimate + 95 % CI per method × quantity), per-fit diagnostics (Rhat, divergences, wall time), and the 1000-draw posterior of \(z\), \(\mathrm{CTmax_{1hr}}\), and \(T_\text{crit}\) for the joint method.
  • per_sim_<label>.rds, meta_<label>.rds, draws_<label>.rds, summary_<label>.rds, diffs_<label>.rds — aggregated tables.

Labels follow the convention sweepA_ub_<sign+abs×1000>, sweepB_u0_<×100>, sweepC_<design>_n<n_reps>.
