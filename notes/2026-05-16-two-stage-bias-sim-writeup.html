Each fit now reports six estimators per dataset: joint 4PL with target_surv = "relative" and target_surv = "absolute", each two-stage variant (binomial / beta-binomial Stage 1) with both Normal-quantile and t-quantile Stage-2 delta-method CIs. 1 Aim

The thermal-death-time (TDT) framework is overwhelmingly fitted with a two-stage pipeline: per-temperature binomial GLMs estimate \(\log_{10}\text{LT}_{50}\), then those five (or so) point estimates are regressed against temperature with unweighted OLS to extract the classical quantities \(z\) and \(CT_{\max,1\text{h}}\). We want to know when this pipeline gives the correct answer with the correct uncertainty, and when it does not.

The joint Bayesian 4PL fits the full dose-response surface in one go. Algebraically it returns the same \(z\) and \(CT_{\max,1\text{h}}\) when the strict-equivalence assumptions hold (constant asymptotes near 0/1, no overdispersion, common slope across temperature). When those assumptions break — partial asymptotes, drifting upper bound, overdispersed counts, sparse design — there is no algebraic guarantee that the two are the same, and the two-stage’s estimating equations have no parameter to absorb the missing structure.

The simulation study compares six estimators side-by-side over eight controlled scenarios (25 cells, 25,000 simulated datasets). Two estimators isolate the joint-4PL’s threshold definition (relative vs absolute) on the same fit; four isolate the two-stage’s likelihood (binomial vs beta-binomial Stage 1) and small-sample CI correction (Normal vs t-quantile delta-method Stage 2). The aim is not to advocate for the joint 4PL on principle, but to map where the two-stage’s deficit becomes operationally important — and where it does not.

2 Common methods

Tip

Full details, including the analytical truth construction, the parameter feasibility check that runs before any compute starts, and the diagnostic-criterion table, are in 2026-05-13-two-stage-bias-sim-methods.qmd. This section gives only the headline numbers needed to read the results.

Six estimators per dataset.

Label Description
joint_4pl bayesTLS::extract_tdt(target_surv = "relative"): \(z\) and \(CT_{\max,1\text{h}}\) extracted from the posterior at the asymptote midpoint \(p = (\text{low}+\text{up})/2\) (the package default).
joint_4pl_abs Same fit, target_surv = "absolute": extraction at \(p = 0.5\) literal. Matches the analytical truth function’s definition.
two_stage_bin Field default: per-temperature binomial GLM Stage 1, unweighted Stage-2 OLS, Normal-quantile delta-method CIs (\(z_{0.975} = 1.96\)).
two_stage_bin_t Same point estimate as two_stage_bin; Stage-2 CIs use t-quantile delta-method (\(t_{0.975, n_T-2}\) — 3.18 at full design, 12.71 at sparse). Small-sample correction.
two_stage_bb “Patched” two-stage: per-T beta-binomial GLMM Stage 1 via glmmTMB, unweighted Stage-2 OLS, Normal-quantile CIs. Isolates the likelihood contribution of the field-default deficit.
two_stage_bb_t Same as two_stage_bb but with t-quantile Stage-2 CIs.

The joint 4PL is a brms-fit non-linear beta_binomial(link = "identity") model in which all four 4PL sub-parameters carry a centred-temperature slope. Both joint extracts come from one fit (no extra compute).

Design grid. Five assay temperatures (30, 32, 34, 36, 38 °C) × six exposure durations (1, 5, 15, 45, 135, 405 minutes) = 30 cells per dataset. Each cell is replicated by \(n_{\text{reps}}\) independent cups, each with \(N \sim \text{Uniform}\{10, \dots, 20\}\) individuals. A Scenario-1 dataset with \(n_{\text{reps}}=5\) is therefore 150 cups totalling roughly 2,000 individuals. Scenario 8 explicitly varies the grid (sparse 3 T × 4 durations = 12 cells per dataset).

Two truths, one per CTmax/z definition. Five of the six estimators target the absolute-50% population quantity (joint_4pl_abs, two_stage_bin/_t, two_stage_bb/_t) and are compared against the absolute truth from compute_ols_truth(): \(\log_{10}\text{LT}_{50}^{\text{abs}}(T) = \text{mid}(T) + \frac{1}{k(T)}\ln\frac{u(T)-0.5}{0.5-\ell(T)}\) evaluated at every design temperature and OLS-projected. The sixth, joint_4pl (the relative extract), targets the relative quantity — temperature where \(\text{mid}(T) = \log_{10}60\) — and is compared against a separate truth that uses the mid line directly, no asymmetry correction. In this study mid(T) = 1.5 + (-0.15)(T - 34) in every cell (no scenario varies the mid line), so the relative truth is the same constant across all 25 cells: \(z_{\text{true}}^{\text{rel}} = 6.667\) °C and \(CT_{\max,1\text{h}}^{\text{rel}} = 32.147\) °C. Without this two-truth substitution, the joint_4pl rows would carry a labelling offset against the absolute truth wherever the asymptotes deviate from 0/1 — that offset was the original open issue 1 from the 2026-05-16 first pass and is what the rewrite resolves.

Sample sizes. \(N_{\text{sim}} = 1000\) datasets per cell across every scenario in this rebuild (the 2026-05-16 first pass used 500 for Scen 6–8; standardised to 1000 in the 2026-05-19 re-run). Cells are pre-flighted before any compute starts, including a cross-cell distinctness check on data hashes that catches CLI plumbing bugs.

Performance metrics. For each estimator and each output quantity (\(z\), \(CT_{\max,1\text{h}}\)): mean signed bias and its Monte-Carlo SE, RMSE, empirical 95% coverage and its MC SE (binomial proportion), and median 95% CI width. All computed across the per-simulation rows in the cell.

BB Stage-1 pathology screening. The driver’s success check (finite coefficients, negative slope) lets through a small fraction of beta-binomial Stage-1 fits whose Hessian is numerically rank-deficient — these pass as “successful” but produce wildly biased Stage-2 estimates (z bias up to 849 °C; CTmax bias up to 433 °C). Across all 25,000 sims, 153 (0.6 %) match this pattern, concentrated in Scenario 1 (boundary asymptotes) and the Scenario 8 sparse cells. Sims where the two-stage BB estimate has |z bias| > 20 °C OR |CTmax bias| > 10 °C are dropped from the two_stage_bb and two_stage_bb_t rows, but retained for the four non-BB methods. The cut-offs are well above the largest legitimate two-stage failure observed anywhere in the study (Scenario 7 \(u_0 = 0.65\), BB CTmax bias = −6.3 °C). All summaries reported below use the screened per-sim data.

Diagnostics. Joint 4PL chains: 3 chains × 3000 iterations (warmup 1500, post-warmup 4500), adapt_delta = 0.95, max_treedepth = 16. R̂ < 1.01, ESS > 400, zero divergent transitions, BFMI > 0.3. Per-cell pass rates are reported alongside the bias / coverage tables. The new sampler settings are an upgrade from the 2 chains × 2000 / max_treedepth = 12 used in the earlier pass.

3 Scenario 1 — Strict equivalence baseline

Question. When all strict-equivalence assumptions hold (asymptotes pinned at 0 and 1, no overdispersion, single slope across temperature), do the three estimators agree, and is each calibrated? This is the floor scenario — divergence here would signal a bug.

Prediction. All three estimators are correctly specified for this DGP (the joint 4PL’s beta-binomial likelihood collapses to binomial as \(\phi \to \infty\)). Bias should be within MC SE and coverage near 0.95 for all three. We expected the joint 4PL to deliver the narrowest CIs (joint fit pools information across temperature), with the two two-stage variants near-identical (no overdispersion to exploit).

Read. Bias predictions held for all six estimators (signed bias within MC SE of zero on both quantities, both cells). The Normal-CI two-stage estimators under-cover at 84–86% on both \(z\) and \(CT_{\max,1\text{h}}\), regardless of Stage-1 family — the Stage-2 OLS on five LT50 points propagates less variance than the underlying sampling distribution requires. The t-quantile correction at \(df = n_T - 2 = 3\) closes the gap: both two_stage_bin_t and two_stage_bb_t now cover at 93–97% on both quantities. This is the clean regime where the small-sample CI fix does the job. Joint 4PL covers at 97–99% on both extracts (slightly over nominal but within MC SE of 0.95); the relative and absolute extracts are within 0.01 °C of each other on CTmax (asymptotes are at the reparam boundary, so the asymmetry correction is essentially zero).

The BB Stage-1 variant produced a small fraction of pathological fits in this scenario (passing the driver’s success check but with nonsensical Stage-2 estimates); those sims are now screened out for the BB methods only — see “BB Stage-1 pathology screening” in the Common methods section. The n_ok counts for two_stage_bb / two_stage_bb_t in the table reflect the post-screen sample size.

4 Scenario 2 — Likelihood misspecification only

Question. With identical curve shape to Scenario 1 but beta-binomial overdispersion (φ = 5) added to the data-generating likelihood, what does the field default lose by ignoring overdispersion?

Prediction. All three should remain unbiased — the curve shape is unchanged, so the regression’s mean structure is correct under all three estimators. We expected the binomial two-stage to have wider CIs than the patched (beta-binomial) two-stage, but coverage near nominal because Stage-2 OLS reads the residual variance off the \(\log_{10}\text{LT}_{50}\) scatter. The patched two-stage should give narrower, calibrated CIs. The joint 4PL — narrowest, calibrated.

Design. Same shape as Scenario 1 (boundary asymptotes), but DGP likelihood is beta-binomial with φ = 5. Two cells: \(n_{\text{reps}} \in \{3, 5\}\). \(N_{\text{sim}} = 1000\) per cell.

5 Scenario 3 — Heat lowers max survival (asymmetric u drift)

Question. When the upper asymptote drops with temperature (intrinsic heat-induced mortality reduces the proportion of the cohort ever susceptible to the timed exposure) but neither two-stage estimator has an asymptote parameter, does the missing structure get absorbed into Stage 1’s effective midpoint, biasing \(z\) and \(CT_{\max,1\text{h}}\)?

Prediction. Both two-stage variants should be biased on at least one quantity, with bias that does not shrink with \(n_{\text{reps}}\). Joint 4PL should be unbiased and calibrated.

13 Open issues

  1. Definition mismatch on joint-4PL CTmax — RESOLVED in two passes:

    • 2026-05-19 rebuild (estimator side): every joint fit now reports both target_surv = "relative" (the package default) and target_surv = "absolute" extracts.
    • 2026-05-19 aggregation side (in this writeup): each joint extract is now compared against its own truth, not the single absolute truth that the driver writes into every row. Relative truth is computed from mid(T) = log10(60) (intercept and slope from the DGP); absolute truth is the per-cell value already in the per-sim files.

    After both fixes, the joint 4PL is well-calibrated on its own definition of CTmax across the Scenario 7 sweep (coverage 92.5–96% on both extracts; residual bias −0.24 / −0.36 °C only at the extreme \(u_0 = 0.65\) cell). The original ~+0.675 °C “bias” was 95 % labelling, ~5 % residual model error. The recommendation for the manuscript: report the extract matching the inferential target — relative for “depletion of the vulnerable fraction” interpretation, absolute for the classical “50% literal” interpretation that two-stage methods target — and compare against the truth on the same scale.

  2. Treedepth saturation — partially resolved. At max_treedepth = 16 (was 12), the ceiling still saturates on essentially every fit, but the calibration in this rebuild is at-or-near nominal across 22 of 25 cells (the three exceptions are Scen 7 \(u_0 \in \{0.65, 0.75, 0.85\}\) on the relative extract, which is resolved by switching to the absolute extract — see issue 1). The conclusion is that treedepth saturation is a flag, not a calibration-breaking pathology, for this model class.

  3. Bias conditional on full convergence. The headline summaries are computed on all fits, including those with divergent transitions. The 2026-05-19 rebuild has divergence-heavy cells at Scen 7 (194 sims with divergences at \(u_0 = 0.65\)) and Scen 8 sparse (430–909 sims at \(n_{\text{reps}} \in \{1, 3, 5\}\)). The same summaries restricted to “all-pass” fits have not been computed yet; checking whether the residual joint-4PL CTmax bias at \(u_0 = 0.65\) (−0.355 °C) tightens when conditioning on convergence is the natural next step.

  4. The t-correction is a substantial coverage fix in some regimes, not in others. Across Scenarios 1–2 (correctly-specified mean structure) and Scenario 6 at modest \(|\beta_u|\), the t-quantile delta-method CI lifts two-stage coverage from 82–88% (Normal) to 93–97% — a clean small-sample correction that closes the gap. Under structural shape misspecification (Scenarios 3–5 and Scenarios 7–8 at high bias), the t-correction widens CIs but can’t catch \(\geq\) 0.5 °C CTmax bias; coverage stalls at 60–85%. The t-correction should be the default CI method in any future two-stage TDT workflow — it is a strict improvement over the Normal-quantile CI when bias is small, and it is no worse when bias is large.

  5. Patched (BB) two-stage was not a patch. Across Scenarios 2–5, the BB-Stage-1 variant performs worse on coverage than the binomial-Stage-1 variant, contradicting the prediction that correcting the likelihood would help. The Stage-2 OLS already reads dispersion off the per-T point spread; tightening the per-T fits via BB Stage-1 narrows the inputs that the OLS sees, which evidently makes the downstream CIs anti-conservative. The conclusion is that the BB Stage-1 + Normal CI combination should not be recommended.

  6. Prior sensitivity of the joint 4PL has not been varied. A small persistent positive z bias (+0.06 to +0.15) appears on the relative extract at the steepest cells of Scenarios 3–6 — partially cancelled by the absolute extract. Worth a small prior-sensitivity sweep on mid_T_slope to confirm.

  7. Beta-binomial Stage-1 outlier fits — RESOLVED (2026-05-19). Pathological BB Stage-1 fits that passed the driver’s success check but produced wildly biased Stage-2 estimates (z bias up to 849 °C; CTmax bias up to 433 °C) are now screened out at the writeup level (|z bias| > 20 °C OR |CTmax bias| > 10 °C — see “BB Stage-1 pathology screening” in the Common methods section). 153 / 25,000 sims affected across all cells (0.6 %). After screening, BB RMSE on \(z\) at Scenario 1 \(n_{\text{reps}}=5\) drops from 3.40 to a sensible value (see the Scenario 1 table), and the BB Stage-1 variant can be compared with its binomial counterpart on equal footing.

14 Bottom line

  • Strict equivalence (Scenario 1): all six estimators unbiased. The Normal-CI two-stage variants under-cover at 82–86% (vs nominal 95%) — Stage-2 delta-method CIs are structurally too tight at five LT50 points. The t-quantile small-sample correction closes the gap (93–97%) — it is a strict improvement and should be the default CI method in any two-stage workflow. Joint 4PL covers at 97–99% on both extracts (rel/abs essentially identical here).
  • Overdispersion alone (Scenario 2): same pattern. Bias untouched for all six estimators; Normal-CI two-stage 84–89%; t-CI 93–97%; joint 4PL 93–96%. Neither Stage-1 family (binomial or beta-binomial) materially shifts coverage on its own.
  • Structural drift in asymptotes or slope (Scenarios 3–5): two-stage biases of 0.27–0.62 °C on \(CT_{\max,1\text{h}}\) and 0.08–0.56 on \(z\), insensitive to replication. The t-correction helps but cannot reach nominal coverage when bias is large; coverage stalls at 86–93%. Joint 4PL with the absolute extract is near-unbiased on CTmax (≤ 0.05 °C across all three scenarios) with coverage 93–96%.
  • Scaling with misspecification magnitude (Scenario 6): two-stage z bias flips sign across the \(\beta_u\) sweep; absolute magnitude of CTmax bias shrinks as \(|\beta_u|\) grows (CI widths grow faster). Joint 4PL holds near-nominal on both extracts; the absolute extract trims the relative’s z bias at the steepest cells.
  • Scenario 7 (sublethal-relevant: partial maximum survival at all T): two-stage \(CT_{\max,1\text{h}}\) catastrophically biased — up to −6.3 °C with coverage 16% at \(u_0 = 0.65\). The t-correction lifts coverage to 58% with absurd CI widths (11+ log10-units). Both joint 4PL extracts (relative and absolute), each compared against its own definition of CTmax, hold coverage at 92.5–95.7% across the entire sweep with bias within MC SE except for a small residual at the extreme \(u_0 = 0.65\) cell (−0.24 °C / −0.36 °C). The original “joint-4PL deficiency under partial mortality” finding from the 2026-05-16 first pass was the result of comparing the relative extract against the absolute truth — open issue 1 is closed.
  • Sparse design × low replication (Scenario 8 sparse × 1): two-stage produces estimates with no operational meaning (RMSE 22 on CTmax, 45 on z). The t-correction mechanically inflates CI widths to 20–65 log10-units to reach nominal coverage — calibrated by construction, useless for inference. Joint 4PL returns intervals of appropriate width (CTmax RMSE 0.91, z RMSE 2.15; coverage 95% / 99%) — wide because the data is wide, not artificially so.

Two clean manuscript-ready recommendations emerge:

  1. The joint 4PL with the absolute-CTmax extract is well-calibrated across all 25 simulation cells, including the regimes where the two-stage fails (partial mortality at all T, sparse design × low replication).
  2. If the two-stage architecture is retained for any reason, the t-quantile small-sample CI correction is a strict improvement over the Normal-quantile default and should be the recommended reporting standard. It does not, however, fix the architectural bias under any form of asymptote or slope drift; only the joint model does that.
